    biography

    I’m a PhD Candidate at the Department of Spanish and Portuguese and Indigenous Languages Graduate Research Assistant at the Lozano Long Institute for Latin American Studies at the University of Texas at Austin. My research focuses on Latin American Indigenous Languages, Literatures, and Cultures, with a focus in Central America, Guatemala, and Maya K’iche’ in particular. I am one of the main creators of Chqeta’maj le Qach’ab’al K’iche’! Let’s learn K’iche’! - an open-source, online course of K’iche’ available at https://tzij.coerll.utexas.edu/.

    I also read and write poetry. I am a member of the Latino Writers Collective of Kansas City, the Taller Literario Don Chico in San José, and the board of directors of Borderlands: Texas Poetry Review in Austin, Texas.
